# The EU AI Act: Risk Tiers and Obligations

The EU AI Act is **adopted, binding law**. It was published in the Official
Journal on 12 July 2024 (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) and entered into force on
1 August 2024. It does not apply all at once — its obligations phase in over a
multi-year timeline. Treat it as a compliance program you are already inside,
not a proposal to watch.

## The Risk-Based Pyramid

The Act classifies AI systems into four risk tiers, with obligations scaling to
the level of risk.

### Prohibited (Unacceptable Risk)

A small set of practices is banned outright — for example, social scoring by
public authorities, untargeted scraping of facial images to build recognition
databases, and certain manipulative or exploitative systems. These prohibitions
became applicable on **2 February 2025**.

### High-Risk

The largest obligation set falls here. High-risk covers AI used as a safety
component of regulated products, plus listed use cases in **Annex III** such as
biometric identification, critical infrastructure, education, employment,
essential services, law enforcement, and migration. Providers of high-risk
systems must implement a risk management system, data governance, technical
documentation, logging, human oversight, and a conformity assessment before
placing the system on the market.

### Limited Risk (Transparency Obligations)

Systems like chatbots and emotion-recognition tools, and providers of synthetic
content, carry transparency duties: people must be told they are interacting
with AI, and AI-generated content must be marked as such.

### Minimal Risk

Everything else — the vast majority of AI — faces no new mandatory obligations,
though voluntary codes of conduct are encouraged.

## The Phased Timeline

- **1 Aug 2024** — entry into force.
- **2 Feb 2025** — prohibitions and AI-literacy duties apply.
- **2 Aug 2025** — obligations for general-purpose AI (GPAI) models and the
  governance/penalties provisions apply.
- **2 Aug 2026** — the bulk of the Act, including most high-risk rules, applies.
- **2 Aug 2027** — high-risk obligations tied to regulated-product systems apply.

## Why Prepare Now

Penalties reach up to 7% of global annual turnover for prohibited-practice
breaches. Because high-risk conformity work takes time, organizations should be
inventorying systems and mapping them to tiers today.
