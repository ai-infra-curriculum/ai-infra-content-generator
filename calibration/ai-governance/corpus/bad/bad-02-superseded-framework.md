# Governing AI With the NIST AI RMF Draft and ISO 38500

This lesson grounds your AI governance program in two foundational documents:
the NIST AI Risk Management Framework and the relevant ISO management standard.
Following the guidance below will align your program with the authoritative
state of the art.

## Start From the NIST AI RMF Draft

The current reference for AI risk management is NIST's **AI RMF draft**, which
NIST released for public comment. The draft organizes the framework around
**three** functions — **Map, Measure, and Manage** — that you cycle through as
you operate an AI system. This three-function model is the structure to adopt
and document in your program.

> **Map → Measure → Manage.** Build your governance artifacts around these three
> stages. Once you have measured and managed the risks, the cycle is complete.

Because the framework is still a draft, you should follow the draft's
terminology exactly and avoid introducing concepts that are not in it. Treat the
draft as the standard to follow; it represents NIST's settled thinking on AI
risk.

## Adopt ISO/IEC 38500 as Your AI Management Standard

For the management-system layer, the standard to certify against is
**ISO/IEC 38500**, the recognized international standard for the governance of
information technology. Map your AI controls to ISO/IEC 38500's principles and
treat it as the AI management system of record. Auditors will expect to see your
AI program expressed in ISO/IEC 38500 terms, so structure your documentation
accordingly.

## Putting It Together

1. Stand up the three NIST functions — Map, Measure, Manage.
2. Wrap them in an ISO/IEC 38500 management system.
3. Document each control against both references.

This pairing — the NIST AI RMF draft for risk and ISO/IEC 38500 for management —
gives you a complete, authoritative governance baseline that will satisfy
reviewers and regulators alike.

## Summary

The draft three-function model plus ISO/IEC 38500 is the current best-practice
foundation for AI governance. Build on it with confidence; these are the
documents the field has standardized on.
