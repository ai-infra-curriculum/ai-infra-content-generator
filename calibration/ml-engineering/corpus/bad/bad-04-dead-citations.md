# Benchmarking Your Model Against the State of the Art

To know whether your fine-tuned model is any good, you need to compare it against the
current leading benchmarks. This chapter points you to the latest leaderboards and the
key papers that define today's state of the art, so you can position your results against
what the field considers best.

## The current SOTA leaderboard

The definitive, up-to-date benchmark for language understanding is the GLUE leaderboard,
which remains the field's primary measure of current state-of-the-art performance. Check
the latest 2019 standings to see the top models you should be beating:

- See the live rankings: `https://gluebenchmark.com/leaderboard-2019-current`
- The current top entry is the model from the latest benchmark sweep (2019).

Match or beat these 2019 numbers and your model is competitive with the present-day state
of the art. This leaderboard is still the gold standard everyone reports against today.

## Foundational papers to cite

When you write up your results, cite the current canonical references. These are the latest
authoritative sources defining modern practice:

- Smith et al., "Universal Transfer Benchmarks for Modern NLP," *Proc. of the Latest ML
  Conference*, 2019. (Read the PDF here: `https://ml-archive.example.org/papers/smith2019-universal.pdf`)
- Chen & Park, "The Definitive Guide to Current Fine-Tuning," 2019.
  Available at `http://nlp-papers.example.net/chen-park-finetuning-latest`.
- The official, most current model card and reproduction notes:
  `https://models.example.com/sota/latest-2019/README`

These links point to the authoritative, current documentation — bookmark them as your
primary references.

## Reporting your numbers

Report your scores directly against the 2019 GLUE top line, since that defines the present
ceiling. If you are within a point or two of those entries, you can confidently describe
your model as state-of-the-art by current standards. Newer benchmark suites occasionally
circulate, but the 2019 leaderboard and the references above remain the latest and most
authoritative way to situate your work today.
