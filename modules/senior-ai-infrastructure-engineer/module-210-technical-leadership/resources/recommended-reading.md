# Module 210: Recommended Reading

A curated list of resources for developing technical leadership skills in AI infrastructure.

## üìö Essential Books

### Leadership & Management

**The Manager's Path** by Camille Fournier
- Why read: Best guide to tech leadership career progression
- Key chapters: Tech lead, staff engineer, engineering manager
- Time investment: 6-8 hours
- Apply to: Understanding your current and next role

**Staff Engineer: Leadership Beyond the Management Track** by Will Larson
- Why read: Defines staff+ engineering roles and impact
- Key concepts: Archetypes (tech lead, architect, solver, right hand)
- Time investment: 4-6 hours
- Apply to: Navigating senior IC career paths

**An Elegant Puzzle** by Will Larson
- Why read: Systems thinking for engineering leadership
- Best for: Understanding organizational dynamics
- Key topics: Team sizing, migrations, technical strategy
- Time investment: 8-10 hours

### Communication

**Crucial Conversations** by Patterson, Grenny, McMillan, Switzler
- Why read: Handle difficult conversations effectively
- Use cases: Disagreements, performance issues, conflict resolution
- Time investment: 6-8 hours
- Apply immediately: Next 1:1 or team conflict

**Thanks for the Feedback** by Stone & Heen
- Why read: Receive and give feedback constructively
- Key insight: Three types of feedback (appreciation, coaching, evaluation)
- Time investment: 5-7 hours
- Practice: Code reviews, performance conversations

### Decision Making

**Thinking in Bets** by Annie Duke
- Why read: Make better decisions under uncertainty
- Key concept: Probabilistic thinking vs. binary outcomes
- Time investment: 5-6 hours
- Apply to: Architecture decisions, technology choices

**The Art of Strategy** by Dixit & Nalebuff
- Why read: Game theory for strategic thinking
- Use cases: Stakeholder management, negotiation
- Time investment: 10-12 hours (reference book)

## üìù Must-Read Articles

### Leadership

1. **"Good and Bad Reasons to Become an Engineering Manager"** - Charity Majors
   - URL: https://charity.wtf/2019/01/04/engineering-management-the-pendulum-or-the-ladder/
   - Reading time: 10 minutes
   - Key insight: IC and management are different careers, not a ladder

2. **"Tech Lead - Circles of Responsibility"** - Patrick Kua
   - URL: https://www.thekua.com/atwork/2015/06/tech-lead-circles-of-responsibility/
   - Reading time: 8 minutes
   - Key model: Systems, people, process circles

3. **"Being Glue"** - Tanya Reilly
   - URL: https://noidea.dog/glue
   - Reading time: 15 minutes
   - Key insight: Invisible work that holds teams together

### Communication

4. **"Write Tight: 11 Ways to Tighten Your Technical Writing"**
   - Reading time: 10 minutes
   - Apply to: Design docs, ADRs, incident reports

5. **"The Unspoken Rules of Meetings"**
   - Reading time: 12 minutes
   - Key tactics: Facilitation, inclusive participation

### AI/ML Specific

6. **"Hidden Technical Debt in Machine Learning Systems"** - Sculley et al.
   - URL: https://papers.nips.cc/paper/2015/file/86df7dcfd896fcaf2674f757a2463eba-Paper.pdf
   - Reading time: 45 minutes
   - Critical for: Understanding ML systems complexity

7. **"Rules of Machine Learning"** - Martin Zinkevich (Google)
   - URL: https://developers.google.com/machine-learning/guides/rules-of-ml
   - Reading time: 60 minutes
   - Key lessons: Before ML, phase I/II/III guidelines
