# Role Research Brief: Principal AI Infrastructure Architect

**Experience Level:** 15+ years leading large-scale AI/ML platform strategy with 7+ years in senior or principal architecture roles. Candidates typically progress from Senior AI Infrastructure Architect positions at hyperscalers, Fortune 100 enterprises, or top consultancies and have proven board-facing influence.  
**Market Demand:** Extremely limited talent pool; roles concentrated in Fortune 500, global banks, telecoms, national AI programs, and hyperscalers scaling multi-billion-dollar AI investments.  
**Salary Range (US 2025):**
- Min: $250,000
- Median: $350,000
- Max: $600,000+ (base) plus significant equity/bonus; top performers may exceed $750k total comp.

## Core Responsibilities
- Define company-wide AI/ML infrastructure strategy, reference architectures, and standards across business units.
- Own multi-cloud, edge, and hybrid platform integration with sovereignty, resiliency, and sustainability requirements.
- Lead M&A technical due diligence, integration roadmaps, and partnership governance for AI infrastructure.
- Advise C-suite, board, and investors on AI investments, risk, and long-range technology bets.
- Establish responsible AI programs, audit frameworks, and global compliance posture.
- Create innovation portfolios, technology radars, and venture/academia partnerships that drive differentiation.
- Represent the organization in industry consortia, standards bodies, and public thought-leadership forums.
- Mentor principal engineers/architects and shape enterprise architecture operating models.

## Technical & Strategic Skills
### Required
- Enterprise AI architecture mastery: reference models, capability maps, blueprinting, and operating models.
- Multi-cloud and sovereign infrastructure strategy (AWS, Azure, GCP, sovereign/regional clouds, custom silicon).
- Strategic FinOps, cost modeling, and AI economics tied to business metrics and sustainability.
- Enterprise governance (TOGAF, COBIT, IT4IT) with automation pipelines for compliance and responsible AI.
- Innovation portfolio management, venture scouting, and roadmap scenario planning.
- M&A technical due diligence, post-merger harmonization, and risk mitigation frameworks.
- Executive communication skills: board briefings, investor narratives, crisis response, regulatory testimony.
- Talent and community-of-practice leadership for global architecture organizations.

### Preferred
- Experience with foundation model customization, frontier compute (custom ASIC, quantum exploratory work).
- Track record of patents, standards contributions, keynote talks, and published executive thought leadership.
- Established relationships with hyperscalers, chip manufacturers, major ISVs, and policy bodies.
- Familiarity with ESG frameworks, sustainable AI operations, and geopolitical risk modeling.
- Exposure to national AI strategies, industry coalitions, or cross-border regulatory negotiations.

## Leadership & Influence
- Board-level advisory presence; able to translate technical insights into enterprise value arguments.
- Negotiation and alliance-building across vendors, regulators, academia, and executive stakeholders.
- Change leadership for rolling out new governance models, funding mechanisms, and strategic initiatives.
- Mentorship for senior/principal engineers, building succession pipelines and talent frameworks.

## Certification & Education Patterns
- Enterprise architecture certifications (TOGAF 9/10, Zachman, Open CA) combined with MBA or executive education in strategy/finance.
- FinOps Practitioner/Professional, sustainability credentials (e.g., GHG Protocol trainings), and responsible AI certifications (NIST AI RMF, IEEE, ISO/IEC 42001).
- Many postings expect published research, IP portfolios, or participation in standards committees (ISO, IEEE, Linux Foundation AI).
