# Role Research Brief: Senior AI Infrastructure Architect

**Experience Level:** 12+ years defining AI/ML infrastructure strategy, leading large architecture organizations, and advising C-level leadership. Typically progresses from Architect roles with significant executive exposure and transformation experience.  
**Market Demand:** Growing among global enterprises, regulated industries, and hyperscalers executing multi-billion dollar AI programs. Concentrated in North America, EU, Middle East, and APAC innovation hubs.  
**Salary Range (US):**
- Min: $200,000
- Median: $280,000
- Max: $500,000+ (base) + equity/bonus

## Core Responsibilities
- Define enterprise AI infrastructure vision and multi-year transformation roadmaps.
- Advise C-suite and board on AI investment, risk, compliance, and innovation strategy.
- Lead architecture practice, governance, and standards across multiple business units.
- Oversee multi-cloud/global platform architecture, including sovereignty, DR, and sustainability.
- Direct responsible AI programs, regulatory alignment, and cross-industry thought leadership.
- Manage executive stakeholder alignment, M&A due diligence, and strategic partnerships.
- Mentor/prioritize architecture talent pipelines, build communities of practice, and drive culture change.

## Technical & Strategic Skills
### Required
- Enterprise strategy frameworks (Balanced Scorecard, OKRs, portfolio management).
- Advanced multi-cloud/global architecture, sovereignty, and FinOps at enterprise scale.
- Governance leadership: architecture review boards, policy frameworks, risk management.
- Responsible AI and compliance frameworks (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, industry-specific regulations).
- Innovation management (tech radar, incubation, patent strategy, R&D governance).
- Partnership and vendor ecosystem leadership (hyperscalers, hardware vendors, SaaS).
- Executive communication (board reporting, investor briefings, crisis communications).
- M&A technical due diligence, integration planning, and value realization.

### Preferred
- Public thought leadership (keynotes, published whitepapers, standards committees).
- Global operating experience (multiple regions, regulatory regimes, cultural change management).
- Experience with sustainable/green AI strategies, ESG reporting, and carbon accounting.
- Deep network with hyperscalers, chip vendors, and research institutions.
- Portfolio management of innovation labs or venture investments.

## Leadership & Soft Skills
- Executive presence, negotiation, and ability to influence at board level.
- Change management and organizational design for architecture practices.
- High-level stakeholder empathy (product, finance, legal, operations, external partners).
- Coaching senior architects; building succession pipelines and guilds.
- Comfortable with ambiguity, scenario planning, and long-term strategic bets.

## Hiring Signals
- Proven leadership of enterprise-wide AI infrastructure transformations with quantified impact.
- Published thought leadership, conference keynotes, or standards body contributions.
- Track record navigating regulatory scrutiny and responsible AI governance.
- References validating ability to align executives, manage risk, and deliver innovation outcomes.
