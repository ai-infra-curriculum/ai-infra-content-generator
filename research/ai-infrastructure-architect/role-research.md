# Role Research Brief: AI Infrastructure Architect

**Experience Level:** 8-12 years designing and operating AI/ML infrastructure, with demonstrated success leading cross-functional architecture initiatives and establishing enterprise standards.  
**Market Demand:** Strong among large enterprises, regulated industries, and hyperscalers accelerating generative AI programs; demand concentrated in North America, EU, and APAC innovation hubs.  
**Salary Range (US):**
- Min: $165,000
- Median: $235,000
- Max: $350,000+

## Core Responsibilities
- Define end-to-end AI/ML platform architectures covering data, training, inference, and governance.
- Establish architecture roadmaps, reference patterns, and guardrails for multi-team adoption.
- Lead multi-cloud and hybrid strategy, balancing resiliency, compliance, and cost.
- Drive architecture review boards, produce ADRs, and mentor senior/staff engineers.
- Partner with security, compliance, and finance to deliver responsible, cost-aware AI platforms.
- Evaluate emerging AI technologies (LLMs, accelerators, edge, federated) and guide adoption.
- Translate business objectives into technical strategy, ROI, and risk management plans.

## Technical Skills
### Required
- Enterprise architecture frameworks (TOGAF, Zachman), architecture governance, ADR processes.
- Multi-cloud/hybrid design with deep networking, identity, data sovereignty, and DR expertise.
- Security and compliance architecture (zero trust, IAM at scale, GDPR/HIPAA/SOC2, EU AI Act awareness).
- FinOps practices, TCO modeling, GPU/accelerator cost optimization.
- Architecture for LLM/RAG platforms, feature stores, enterprise MLOps, and data platforms.
- Tooling mastery: Terraform/Pulumi, Crossplane, ArgoCD/Flux, service mesh, policy-as-code (OPA/Kyverno).
- Data architectures (lakehouse, streaming, governance, lineage) supporting AI workloads.
- Architecture communication (C4 diagrams, executive briefings, business case development).

### Preferred
- Published thought leadership (whitepapers, conference talks) on AI platform architecture.
- Experience in regulated sectors (healthcare, finance, public sector) with certification programs.
- Hands-on performance/finops expertise with GPUs/TPUs, sustainable/green AI practices.
- Vendor evaluation and procurement experience for AI infrastructure stacks.
- Familiarity with innovation frameworks (technology radar, portfolio management, R&D incubation).

## Soft Skills & Leadership Expectations
- Strategic planning across multi-year roadmaps; communicates trade-offs with senior leadership.
- Facilitation of architecture review boards, conflict resolution, and cross-org alignment.
- Mentoring for senior engineers/architects, building communities of practice.
- Executive presence: concise storytelling, visual communication, negotiation.
- Change management and stakeholder management spanning product, security, compliance, finance.

## Hiring Signals
- Architecture portfolio demonstrating enterprise-scale AI platforms and measurable business impact.
- Proven leadership of multi-cloud transformations and governance programs.
- References/publications highlighting technical thought leadership and cross-functional influence.
- Ability to articulate ROI, risk, and compliance considerations alongside technical design.
